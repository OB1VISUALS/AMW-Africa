OUR STORY

In studying the history of revivals, Francis Frangipane noticed something striking: every revival was preceded by an organized prayer movement—except one. Curious, he asked the Lord why. The Lord replied,

“I answered the prayers of a million mothers.”

In 1999, the Lord spoke to Papa Lou Engle, a prophetic father to a generation, with a defining question: "How can I turn America back to God?" That question set the trajectory for the next 25 years of prayer, fasting, and national repentance. In September 2000, after God miraculously provided for the work, 400,000 young adults gathered on the National Mall in Washington, D.C. for a Joel 2-style solemn assembly of fasting and prayer.

In 2024, that legacy crescendoed into the launch of A Million Women (AMW) on October 12, the Day of Atonement. Out of this divine moment, A Million Women Africa (AMWA) was born — under the continental leadership of Apostle Isi Igenegba.

OUR VISION & MISSION

VISION

As a parachurch prayer movement, A Million Women Africa exists to mobilize women and men as modern-day Esthers and Mordecais — calling nations to prayer, fasting, and spiritual awakening for the fulfillment of their prophetic destiny, and to secure a future for the next generation.

MISSION

A Million Women Africa exists to mobilize women and men as modern-day Esthers and Mordecais — awakening nations to prayer, fasting, and spiritual transformation through:

  • — Monthly prayer and training
  • — Annual gatherings centered around the Day of Atonement
  • — National solemn assemblies
